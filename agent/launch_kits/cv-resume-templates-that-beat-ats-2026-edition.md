Most advice on ATS resume templates is either vague or trying to sell you something. So here's the actual workflow I use, free:

1. **Go single-column.** Most ATS parsers read left-to-right across the whole page, so a two-column template with a sidebar gets scrambled. One column, top to bottom, no text boxes, no tables for your work history.
2. **Use boring section headings.** Name them exactly *Work Experience*, *Education*, *Skills*, *Certifications*. Creative headers like "Where I've Made Impact" can make the parser skip the whole section.
3. **Mirror the posting's exact words.** List the repeated nouns in the job description (tools, the exact job title, certifications). Where they're genuinely true of you, use that exact phrasing — "stakeholder management," not "kept everyone in the loop."
4. **Spell out acronyms once:** "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)." Some systems search the acronym, others the full term — covering both matches without keyword stuffing.
5. **Keep contact info in the body, never the header/footer** — some parsers ignore those regions and your email silently disappears. Save as a text-based PDF or .docx, not a scanned image.
