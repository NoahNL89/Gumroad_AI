Most advice on LinkedIn content system is either vague or trying to sell you something. So here's the actual workflow I use, free:

1. Define three content pillars before drafting. For each pillar, write down the audience problem, the outcome you help create, and one experience that proves you understand it. This keeps a month of posts coherent without making them repetitive.
2. Batch five posts in one 90-minute session: choose topics and hooks for 20 minutes, draft without editing for 25 minutes, run one focused edit pass for 15 minutes, then format and schedule. Separating drafting from editing prevents each post from consuming an entire afternoon.
3. Give every calendar slot a hook before you call it planned. Use a specific number, event, or audience in the first line, keep it under 15 words, and read it aloud. A topic without a working first line is still an unfinished idea.
4. In the first hour after publishing, answer every real comment and leave substantive comments on 3–5 relevant posts. Distribution is part of the workflow, not something to remember after writing.
